1. Introduction  Continental Pension Services (CPS) Continental Pension Services Company Limited (CPS) is a licensed Pension Services Company registered with the registrar of financial institutions (Reserve Bank of Malawi).  The company is licensed to operate as a Pension Services Company and provides the following services: a. Carrying out administration of pension funds for restricted and unrestricted pension schemes. b. Acting as a Corporate Trustee of the Unrestricted fund. c. Provider of programmed withdrawals d. Management of minors funds • A Restricted Pension Fund refers to a pension scheme whose membership is limited exclusively to employees of a specific employer. These funds are classified as financial institutions and must be registered with the Registrar of Financial Institutions. • An Unrestricted Pension Fund, on the other hand, accommodates contributions from both companies and individuals who are not independently registered with the Registrar. It also allows for voluntary contributions by individuals in addition to their mandatory monthly contributions. Continental Pension Services intends to procure a comprehensive Back Office System to modernize and automate its core business processes. The aim is to enhance operational efficiency, data accuracy, and regulatory compliance across all pension administration functions. The proposed system will serve as the central platform for managing pension-related activities and will support seamless integration with external applications, such as Sage Accounting Software. The products to be managed by the system are Pension administration, voluntary contributions and Programmed Withdrawal. 2. Document Purpose This Business Requirements Document (BRD) defines the key functional and non-functional requirements of the proposed Back Office System. It is intended to: • Guide potential vendors in understanding the scope of work. • Ensure that vendor proposals are aligned with the strategic and operational goals of Continental Pension Services. • Provide a baseline for system evaluation, development, implementation, and future enhancements. 3. Objectives  The primary objectives of the Back Office System are to: • Streamline client onboarding processes for both restricted and unrestricted pension schemes. • Automated receipting and payment workflows to reduce manual intervention and errors. • Generate detailed and customizable reports to support management decision-making and regulatory compliance. • Enable real-time compliance monitoring aligned with local financial regulations. • Facilitate integration with third-party systems, including accounting 4. Functional Requirement 4.1  Module 1 - Pension Administration 4.1.1 Company and Member Management  This feature should allow the creation and management of companies and individual members who contribute to the pension fund.  Member/Company Registration  • Ability to register with either a company or an individual member • Input fields include o First Name / Company Name – Text input  o Last Name – Required for individual members (not for companies) o Date of Birth / Company Creation Date – Date picker field o Gender – Dropdown (Male, Female, Other) o Date of Joining – Date picker, when the member joined the scheme o Physical Address – Includes location and district o Place of birth-Includes location and district o Village – Optional field for rural-based members o Phone Number – Numeric input with country code support o ID Number and ID Type – Store type (e.g., National ID, Passport, Driver’s License) and the corresponding number • Next of Kin Details: o Full Name o ID Number o Address o Phone Number • Beneficiary Details (support multiple beneficiaries): o Name o Date of Birth o ID Number o Relationship o Benefit Rate/Percentage (%) o Account Status – Options: Active, Dormant, Deceased o Marital Status – Options: Single, Married, Divorced, Widowed • Authorization Levels: o Inputter – Can create records and modify before authorization  o Authorizer – Reviews and approves entries Authorization Workflow The system shall implement a structured authorization workflow to ensure accountability, data integrity, and compliance. Two primary user roles are involved in the workflow: Capturer and Authorizer. • Capturer: Responsible for entering and modifying records prior to submission for approval. Once a record is submitted, the capturer loses edit privileges, ensuring that no changes can be made post-submission. • Authorizer: Responsible for reviewing, approving, or rejecting submitted records. An authorizer must provide a mandatory reason when authorizing or rejecting a transaction. This reason is recorded and maintained as part of the transaction's audit trail. Both roles are permitted to modify records where errors are identified, but such modifications are strictly governed by the following rules: • A capturer can only modify a record before it is submitted for approval. • An authorizer can make modifications during the review process or after approval, provided that all changes are clearly logged in the audit trail with the user’s identity, timestamp, and reason for modification. All actions—including creation, modification, approval, and rejection— shall be captured in a comprehensive audit log to ensure traceability and transparency throughout the system. 4.1.2 Receipts Management  This function should enable users to record contributions or other payments from members or companies. Authorization Levels: • Inputter creates receipt entries. • Authorizer validates and approves them. • Both roles can modify receipts in case of input errors. 4.1.3 Pension Payment Management  Handles the process of disbursing pension benefits to members.
